💡 Critical Findings

Employees who work overtime have a 30.5% attrition rate — nearly 3× the rate of non-overtime employees (10.4%). Reducing mandatory overtime is the single biggest lever for retention.
Employees earning below ₹3,000/month face 41.2% attrition. This salary band, mostly Sales Representatives and Lab Technicians, needs urgent compensation review.
Employees under 25 years old leave at 35.5% — more than double the company average. Early career engagement programs and mentorship can address this gap.
The Sales department (20.6%) and Human Resources (19.0%) are 30–40% above the company average attrition rate of 16.1%.

✅ Recommended HR Actions

Salary Increment Program: Prioritize salary increases for the below-₹3K band. A 20% increment could reduce attrition in that group to ~25%, saving ~14 employees per cycle.
Overtime Policy Reform: Implement a 45-hour weekly cap with mandatory comp-off. Model predicts this reduces high-risk flags by 18%.
Early Tenure Program: Create a "First 3 Years" accelerated track for under-25 hires with mentorship, fast-track promotion criteria, and quarterly check-ins.
Sales Team Intervention: Sales Representatives have both the highest attrition (39.8%) and lowest salary (₹2,626). Role redesign + 15% salary hike + commission restructure recommended.
ML Model Integration: The Flask ML model identifies high-risk employees proactively. HR should review flagged employees monthly and trigger the above interventions before resignation.
